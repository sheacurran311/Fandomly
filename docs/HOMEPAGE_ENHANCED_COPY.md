## Writing Principles Applied

### 1. **Benefit-First Language**
Every feature tied to an outcome:
- "Turn fans into superfans" (not "platform for fans")
- "Watch your value explode" (not "track metrics")

### 2. **Concrete Over Abstract**
- "Game-worn jerseys" (not "physical rewards")
- "60 seconds" (not "quickly")
- "50 brands, 100 campaigns" (not "scale")

### 3. **Rhythm & Repetition**
- "Your brand. Your domain. Your auth."
- "Real actions. Real rewards. Real-time."
- "No delays, no manual checking, no fraud."

### 4. **Power Verbs**
- Explode, Deploy, Soar, Compound
- (not: enhance, improve, optimize)

### 5. **Conversational Tone**
- "Already have authentication? Perfect."
- "Your superfans? They're worth gold."
- "Like having a growth team in your pocket."

### 6. **Remove Hedging**
**Before:** "can help you grow"
**After:** "grows your revenue"

### 7. **Specificity Builds Trust**
- "under 60 seconds" (not "fast")
- "32+ proven templates" (not "many options")

### 8. **Empathy & Pain Points**
- "Streaming pays pennies" (acknowledge frustration)
- "No crypto confusion" (remove barrier)
- "Beyond the algorithms" (common enemy)

## Testing Notes

When testing, pay attention to:
1. **Emotional response** - Does it feel exciting?
2. **Clarity** - Is every feature's benefit obvious?
3. **Credibility** - Do specific numbers/claims feel authentic?
4. **Flow** - Does the page build momentum toward conversion?

---

## Key Metrics to Watch

Post-launch, track:
- Time on page (should increase with compelling copy)
- Scroll depth (expect higher completion rate)
- CTA clicks (hero vs. footer)
- Bounce rate (should decrease)
- Sign-up conversion rate (primary goal)
